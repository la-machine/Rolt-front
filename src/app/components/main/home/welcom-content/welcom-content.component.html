For Lands that doesn't yet have

Land Title

Any person entitled to apply for a land title on a dependence of the national domain must constitute a file including:

  • An application in four (4) copies, the original of which is stamped, indicating the applicantâ€™s full name, parentage, domicile, profession, marital status, nationality, the name under which the property is to be registered.
  • The description of the property (location, surface area, nature of occupation or operation, estimate of its value, indication of the charges on it).
  • Provide a tax clearance certificate from the relevant tax authority to confirm that all applicable taxes related to the land have been paid
  • The mayor then make a public notice informing the public that you requested for a land title on that land
  • The mayor then set a date where the advisory commision will come and investigate on the Land to ascertain whether the applicant occupied the land before August 5, 1974.
  • If the land is highlighted and if it is the applicant who carried out this highlighting, if all of this is positive, the commission proceeds with the establishment of the terminals on the ground (This is done by the surveyor of the advisory commision).
  • If everything when on well the advisory commision then return and make a report which shall be examin for the creation of the land title.

For Lands that already have Land title

Land title Transfer or parcel fragmentation

Notary

  • Get a certificate of ownership: This document will give informations on the present owner of that land title
  • Get certificate of Urbanism: This will give the informations on whether the land belongs to the government (It shows the lands own by the government in that area).
  • Obtain a Survey Plan: Once you have identified the land you wish to acquire, you need to obtain a survey plan from a licensed surveyor. The survey plan shows the size and boundaries of the land.
  • Obtain a Certificate of Non-encumbrance: Its advisable to obtain a certificate of non-encumbrance from the land registry to confirm that there are no outstanding debts or legal claims against the land.
  • Obtain a Transfer Tax Clearance: You must obtain a transfer tax clearance from the tax authorities to confirm that all applicable taxes have been paid.
  • Submit the Application: The seller needs to write an apllication (Transfer or fragmentation) letter. This letter should contain the seller,buyer and notary informations and also the amount agreed to buy the land.
